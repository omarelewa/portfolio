THE RHYME ENDS ON THE ANTHEM

This isn't clever copy. It's a phonetic machine.
  • Every line lands on the same sound, the “-ādī” rhyme.
  • Four different feelings, one hypnotic ending.
  • For about sixteen seconds, your ear gets trained to expect one last word.
  • It resolves on بلادي بلادي, the opening words of Egypt's national anthem (“Bilady, Bilady”).
  • A Syrian house declaring, in two words, that its shawarma is Egypt's own.
The rhyme doesn't end on a word. It ends on the anthem.

Move 1

OWN THE QUESTION, FOREVER

  • Don't spend the question on one launch. Make it the brand's question for a decade.
  • Shift the meaning: not “which flavor?” but “which shawarma are you?”
  • Turn a product question into an identity question, and now you own a feeling instead of a flavor.
  • It runs forever. Every occasion, mood and season is a fresh way to ask it again.

Move 2

MAKE THE JINGLE THE SONG OF THE SUMMER

Keep every word, and lean into the anthem it already echoes.
  • Produce it close to mahraganat, the sound that owns the Egyptian street, but push it anthemic. A proud, chant-along hook.
  • It already ends on بلادي بلادي. Treat that drop like a stadium chant, since everyone already knows the words.
  • Put a real artist on it so it streams and gets stitched. Then pull a 4-note sonic logo out of it and run that everywhere.

Move 4

TURN FIVE SKETCHES INTO ONE ANTHEM

  • Keep all five scenes, but stop running them like a reel.
  • Cut them into one escalating film, one beat per line, timed to the song. It rises from craving to the comedy peak to the tender heart…
  • …and resolving on بلادي بلادي, where the new sandwich drops, the four characters converge, and the anthem swells.
  • Give it the heart it's missing. Bookend it with the brand's own truth: a Syrian house that fell for Egypt and cooked a taste so local it basically became the anthem.
