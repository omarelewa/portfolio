A Lebanese Restaurantbringing Lebanon to the table

Ten Names Three Territories لبنان

Don't name the cuisine. Name the memory of Lebanon.

The Brief

The task: name a Lebanese restaurant, a place of warmth, generosity and deep flavour. The strategy is single-minded. Don't describe "Lebanese food," evoke a feeling of Lebanon itself: the clay oven and the family pot, the lemon groves and mountain herbs, the poetry and percussion of a long Lebanese night. Ten names, sorted into three territories, each a different way to bring Lebanon to the table.

Territory I

Home, Hearth & Table

Names rooted in the Lebanese home: the clay oven, the family pot, the house, and the table everyone gathers around.

01

Bayt El Tannour

بيت التنور

House of the clay oven.

Bayt El Tannour names the beating heart of Lebanese cooking, the tannour, where bread blisters and stews cook low and slow. It promises warmth, craft and the feeling of stepping into a Lebanese home rather than a restaurant. Built for a kitchen that treats fire-and-clay tradition as its signature.

The hook · the clay oven
02

Tanjara

طنجرة

The family pot.

Tanjara is the humble cooking pot at the centre of every Lebanese kitchen, the vessel behind the slow stews and the bubbling yakhne, the food that simply says home. As a name it's warm, unpretentious and quietly confident. It doesn't dress the cuisine up, it celebrates the everyday pot that makes it. Charming, memorable, refreshingly down-to-earth.

The hook · the everyday pot, elevated
03

Beit Karaz

بيت كرز

House of cherries.

Beit Karaz pairs the warmth of home, beit, with the sweetness of the cherries grown in Lebanon's high-altitude orchards. It's tender, nostalgic and lovely on the tongue, evoking seasonal fruit, generous hospitality and natural beauty. Ideal for a restaurant that leads with fresh, seasonal produce and a soft, welcoming spirit.

04

Levantine Table

بلاد الشام

The whole region, on one table.

Levantine Table widens the view from Lebanon to the whole Levant, the shared mezze culture of a region that eats generously and lingers long. Refined, globally legible and instantly understood by an international audience, it signals authenticity while welcoming everyone to the table. The most outward-looking name in the set, for ambitions beyond a single cuisine.

The hook · pan-Levantine, globally legible
Territory II

The Land

Names drawn from Lebanon's terroir: its citrus and orchards, its valleys and snow-capped peaks.

05

Leymoune

ليمون

Lemon, the brightness of the table.

Leymoune captures the bright, citrus snap that runs through Lebanese cooking, from tabbouleh to the tang of a good marinade. Fresh, simple and sunlit, it reads modern and Mediterranean, perfect for a kitchen that leans light, zesty and contemporary. One vivid word that tastes like the food.

06

Jebal

جبال

The mountains of Lebanon.

Jebal, "mountains," honours the snow-capped peaks and fertile valleys that define Lebanon and feed its tables. The name carries strength, height and a deep sense of place, ideal for a restaurant that celebrates terroir and sources from the land. Simple, grounded, quietly majestic.

07

Jabal Rehanne

جبل ريحان

The mountain of wild herbs.

Jabal Rehanne names a real Lebanese mountain, lush, green, scented with the wild basil and herbs that grow across its slopes. It roots the restaurant in a specific, forageable place and the village tradition of cooking with what the land gives. The natural choice for a farm-to-table kitchen built on authenticity and aroma.

The hook · a real mountain, wild herbs
Territory III

Poetry & Celebration

Names that carry Lebanon's lyrical, musical soul: its verse, its reed flute, its rhythm and festivity.

08

Jazal

جَزَل

The eloquence of zajal.

Jazal is the Arabic word for poetic eloquence, the lyrical art behind zajal, Lebanon's beloved tradition of improvised folk verse. The name wraps the restaurant in artistry and storytelling, casting every dish as a line of poetry with deep cultural roots. For a place that wants food to feel like an expression of Lebanon's lyrical soul.

The hook · zajal, Lebanese folk poetry
09

Nay

ناي

The reed flute's song.

Nay is the reed flute whose breathy, soulful sound threads through Lebanese and Levantine music, intimate, ancient and deeply moving. As a name it's elegant and atmospheric, promising a dining room with feeling: candle-soft, melodic, unhurried. Perfect for a restaurant that sells mood and memory as much as a meal.

The hook · the reed flute, intimate
10

Tambourine

Rhythm, joy, the table alive.

Tambourine borrows the bright percussion of Lebanese celebration, the hand-drum pulse behind every wedding and every long table that turns into dancing. The name is all joy and tempo, made for a lively, social, high-energy room where food, music and people collide. A confident, festive choice that promises a good night, not just a good meal.

Where to From Here

A shortlist is a conversation, not a verdict. The strongest routes take something specific about Lebanon, a real mountain, a clay oven, a line of folk verse, and turn it into a feeling you can taste.

Because specificity is what makes a name unforgettable and impossible to copy. Recommended next step: shortlist three, check trademark and domain, see how each reads on a sign and a menu in both Latin and Arabic, and say every one out loud before deciding.
