Naming & Brand Strategy Case Study · Verbal Identity
For Taj City · Cairo

TAJED

تجد

tajid, "you find"

It carries the crown's name. And it quietly tells you what to do once you're inside. Find.

The Outcome
Taj City chose it and built it. Tajed is live in the compound today.
The Brief

Taj City is a premium residential compound in Cairo, and it was opening a new retail spot inside the gates. A strip mall, basically, for the people who already live there. The brief wanted one hard thing from the name. It had to do two jobs at once. Belong to Taj City without question, and make somewhere you shop feel like somewhere you actually want to go.

The Tension

A mall inside a compound is a tricky thing to name. Too generic and it's just "the shops." Too independent and it floats away from the brand that paid for it. So the name had to be of Taj City, borrowing its name and a little of its crown-like prestige, while still standing on its own with some warmth and pull. And this being Cairo, it had to read just as well in Arabic as in English.

The Insight

Forget the retail for a second. A place like this really exists for one verb: to find. You go to find what you need, find something you love, run into your neighbours, find a reason to leave the house on a Friday. The best thing the name could do was promise that, and carry the parent brand while it did it.

The Idea

تجد

tajid, "you find."

Tajed is one word that's invented and real at the same time, and it does both jobs at once. In Arabic, تجد means "you find." Written in Latin letters, it wears the parent right there on its sleeve: Taj-ed. So it says "this belongs to Taj City" and "this is where you find everything" in the same breath. One word, two languages, two gifts.

The Layers
The Parent

Taj, the crown

It opens on Taj. That's Taj City's own name, and the Arabic word for crown. By the first syllable, the place already reads as family, and as premium.

The Promise

"You find" تجد

In Arabic, تجد is the verb every visitor is there to do: you find. The brand promise is baked into the name, no campaign required.

The Invitation

Come and discover

Not "shops," not "mall." It's an open invitation to come and find something. Warm and active, where most retail names just sit there.

The Fusion

One word, both jobs

The link to the parent and the promise to the customer, folded into one word you can own. Meaningful and protectable, which rarely show up together.

Why It Works · The Dimensions
I
Effortless brand architecture

Most names for this kind of place need a crutch. "Taj City Mall." "The Shops at…". Tajed doesn't. Because Taj is already sitting inside it, the endorsement happens on its own. It works alone on a sign and still reads as Taj City's child. The equity carries through without anyone spelling it out.

II
The verb that sells

A destination is where you find things. Name it after that verb and the promise is hard to miss. It works on the practical level ("find everything you need") and the softer one ("find your people, find a good evening"). The name is doing the marketing before the marketing starts.

III
Two languages, two gifts

A bilingual name that isn't a compromise, which is rarer than it sounds. Arabic readers get the meaning: تجد, "you find." Latin readers get the brand: Taj-ed. Both readings work in your favour, and nothing falls apart in translation. In a city that lives in two languages, that counts for a lot.

IV
Coined, yet real

Most invented names are empty. Most meaningful names are generic and impossible to own. Tajed is both at once, a coined word that's distinctive and easy to protect but still carries real meaning. That mix is the hardest thing to pull off in naming, and probably the most valuable.

V
The crown it inherits

Taj means crown. Tajed leans on that quietly. The crowning place to find everything, an address that lives up to the compound's name. The premium feeling is built in rather than bolted on.

VI
Built to grow

It opens up a whole language of finding. "You'll find it." "Everything you're looking for." تجد كل شيء. And it can name wings, seasons and events as the place grows. A name with somewhere to go.

Selected · Implemented · Live

This one isn't a proposal. Taj City chose Tajed and built it, and the name is live in the compound today.

That's usually the sign a name is working. The client saw themselves in it straight away, and a whole destination got an identity that was premium, warm, bilingual and unmistakably theirs. All of it in five letters.
