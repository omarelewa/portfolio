Don't announce a nightclub. Start a rumor, and let the whole city open it for you.

The Idea, in One Breath

We make Cairo believe a mythical creature has slipped out of Arab legend and onto its streets. The city starts whispering. And when the rumor finally breaks, it turns out to be Ava. The launch isn't an ad. It's a magic act, performed across a whole city.

The Strategy
The Brand

Ava is a Magician. Its archetype is enchantment and transformation. "Dreams do come true," the night made alive. Every move the brand makes should feel like a reveal.

The Audience

Cairo's nightlife generation. Twenties and thirties, social, permanently online, ruled by FOMO. They go where the buzz is, share what they see, and dread missing the night everyone's talking about.

The Insight

In Egypt, a rumor outruns the truth. Nothing here travels faster than "I heard that…" So instead of buying attention, we'll start a rumor and let the city market Ava to itself.

The Creative Leap

A magic act begins in mystery and ends in a reveal. So the launch itself becomes the act. First the trick, a creature loose in Cairo. Then the reveal: it was Ava all along.

The Device

Our creature is the Shadhavar الشادهوار, a beast from classical Arab literature. Gazelle-like, with a single hollow, branching horn that sings when the wind moves through it. Mysterious. Beautiful. Musical. Faintly dangerous. A nightclub with hooves. And it belongs to our own culture, so no rival can ever own it.

Positioning

Ava. The night is alive. A night so good you leave and start your own rumor about it.

The Big Idea
The campaign in three words
I Heard a Rumor

Launch Ava as an urban legend, not an advertisement. Seed a city-wide rumor of a mythical singing creature, let it spread on its own, then reveal it as Ava. After that, keep the rumor alive as Ava's permanent Friday-night ritual. One idea that powers the tease, the launch, and every week after.

The Campaign · Three Acts
01

The Rumor

Mystery · about 3 to 4 weeks pre-launch

Objective: pure intrigue. No brand, no logo, just a legend spreading.

Seeded sightings

Influencers and "ordinary" people post cryptic encounters. "I heard a rumor it was running on the Ring Road." "A gazelle-like thing was playing with my dog." Plus blurry shadow clips, hoofprints photographed across the city, and faint melodies recorded at night.

Guerrilla in the streets

Hoof-print trails and stencils appear across nightlife districts. A haunting horn-melody drifts from hidden speakers. Silhouette-only flyposters carry a single QR to a dark teaser page. Little breadcrumbs that, only later, lead toward Ava.

The sound

Release snippets of an original Shadhavar melody, the horn's song, built to become Ava's sonic signature and a shareable sound people lip-sync and reuse.

Measure  reach · shares & saves · follower growth · "what is this?" volume · earned/press pickups · count of UGC sightings.
02

The Reveal

Excitement · launch week

Objective: turn the mystery into Ava, and open the doors.

The turn

The line flips from question to answer: "The night is alive." The rumor breaks. The creature was the spirit of a new club, Ava, and every "sighting" was the city hearing Ava before it opened.

The reveal film

Stitch the sightings together → the Shadhavar walks into Ava → the room ignites → "Ava. The night is alive. Opening [date]." Framed openly as the reveal of a trick: "You heard the rumor. Here's the truth."

Opening night

An immersive event. The Shadhavar as a living installation (a horned performer, aerialist, projection-mapped beast), illusionists, the signature melody as the very first track, an "enter the rumor" threshold. Seat the influencers who spread it, the press, and the people whose clips went viral, and pay off the seeding.

Measure  opening attendance · bookings · reveal-film views · earned-media value · sentiment.
03

Rumor Nights

Always-on · retention engine

Objective: turn a launch into a weekly ritual that feeds itself.

The ritual

"I Heard a Rumor" becomes Ava's Friday. Each week, a new rumor teases what's coming: a secret guest, a theme, a surprise. It keeps the Magician's promise that every night holds a reveal.

The weekly loop

Sunday: drop a cryptic rumor (engagement). Tuesday: reveal it and open bookings. Friday: the night itself. Then Friday's UGC becomes the seed of next Sunday's rumor. A circle that feeds itself.

The audience re-seeds it

Guests leave and become the rumor. "I heard a rumor about last Friday at Ava…" So the city keeps restarting the campaign for us, more or less forever.

Content pillars  the Rumor (tease) · the Reveal (what's on) · the Proof (aftermovies & UGC) · the Legend (Shadhavar lore).

Why It Works
  • It turns Egypt's fastest medium, the rumor, into the media plan. The city does the advertising.
  • The mechanic is the brand. Mystery → reveal → enchantment is a Magician's act, and that's exactly who Ava is.
  • It's culturally ownable. The Shadhavar is real Arab myth and literally musical. Perfect for a club, impossible for a competitor to copy.
  • It's earned-first. Cheap, credible and viral long before a single pound of paid media.
  • It never ends. Rumor Nights makes guests re-seed the rumor every single week.
Handle With Care
The one risk worth managing

A rumor campaign works precisely because it feels real, so keep the legend clearly fantastical and playful. A mythical singing creature is delightful. A believable dangerous-animal scare that could cause real panic or pull in the authorities is not, and that's the line you never cross. Set a tight reveal window so the mystery never curdles into fatigue. Make opening night over-deliver so the trick feels earned. Brief every creator to hold the enchanted tone. And make the reveal visibly thank the city: you made the rumor real.
